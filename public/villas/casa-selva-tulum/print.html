Places
TO EAT
Places TO EAT
HartwoodMaps
Tulum's most celebrated restaurant — all wood-fired, all local. No reservations. Arrive early (opens at 6 PM) or expect a wait. Worth every minute.
ArcaMaps
Contemporary Mexican cuisine in a stunning open-air space. Creative tasting menus using local ingredients. Reservations recommended.
El Camello Jr.Maps
No-frills local seafood at its best — fresh ceviche, grilled fish, cold beers. A true Tulum gem that tourists rarely find.
Places TO DRINK
GitanoMaps
A magical jungle bar with mezcal cocktails, live music, and a setting unlike anywhere else on earth. Sunset is perfect timing.
Batey Mojito & Guarapo BarMaps
Crushed sugarcane mojitos made fresh to order in an open-air shack. Cash only, unforgettable, and impossibly cheap.
Things TO DO
Gran CenoteMaps
The most beautiful cenote near Tulum — crystal-clear water, stalactites, and turtles. Reachable by bicycle in 15 minutes. Arrive before 9 AM to beat the crowds.
Tulum RuinsMaps
Ancient Mayan cliff-top ruins overlooking the turquoise Caribbean. One of the most photogenic archaeological sites in Mexico. Open daily from 8 AM.
Sian Ka'an Biosphere ReserveMaps
UNESCO World Heritage Site just 30 minutes south. Boat tours through mangroves, wildlife spotting, and remote beaches that feel like the edge of the world.
